The builder said he was going to replace this rosette as it was damaged and covered with a century of paint. In the end he didn''t and added more layers of paint.

More astonishing to me was that he had the top coat applied to the walls before the preparation of the window frames was complete. As I recall, he said his men would use a blowtorch to remove the paint but obviously they couldn't go all the way to the edge since on one side they'd risk burning the new windows and on the other risk burning the apricot-colored top coat.
